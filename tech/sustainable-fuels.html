100% Sustainable Fuels

How motorsport is ditching fossil oil without sacrificing performance.

The 2026 Revolution

From 2026, every Formula 1 car will run on fully synthetic, carbon‑neutral fuel. It’s chemically identical to petrol but made from captured CO₂ and renewable hydrogen (e‑fuels) or from agricultural waste (advanced biofuels). These “drop‑in” fuels require no engine modifications, yet their lifecycle CO₂ emissions are net zero.

How E‑fuels are Made

E‑fuels combine green hydrogen (produced by electrolysis using wind or solar power) with carbon dioxide captured directly from the air or industrial processes. The result is a liquid hydrocarbon that burns just like petrol. Porsche, for example, has built a pilot plant in Chile producing 130,000 litres per year and plans to scale up to 550 million litres. Racing serves as the perfect high‑profile testbed.

Biofuels – Waste to Power

Second‑generation biofuels use non‑food biomass: corn stover, forestry waste, even municipal garbage. The process – gasification and Fischer‑Tropsch synthesis – creates high‑purity fuel. The World Rally Championship (WRC) has committed to 100% sustainable fuel from 2025, and the World Endurance Championship (WEC) already allows renewable fuels in all classes.

Why It Matters Beyond Racing

Electrification is not possible for every vehicle. Long‑haul aviation, shipping, and heavy‑duty trucks will rely on liquid fuels for decades. By pushing the development of sustainable fuels, motorsport guarantees that those sectors can also decarbonise. Every litre of sustainable fuel burned in a race car is a litre of fossil fuel left in the ground.
