Sustainable Tyres – More Than Black Rubber

Tyres connect the car to the road, and they’re one of the biggest sustainability challenges in racing. A revolution is underway.

The Problem with Conventional Tyres

Traditional racing tyres use synthetic rubber made from petroleum, and they wear out quickly – generating tonnes of used rubber every race weekend. The carbon black filler is also derived from oil, and steel belts require energy‑intensive mining. Moreover, tyre particulates are a major source of microplastic pollution. Motorsport has begun to tackle these issues head‑on.

Natural Rubber & Guayule

Pirelli, the supplier to Formula 1, has introduced tyres made with natural rubber from the guayule shrub. Guayule grows on arid land not suitable for food crops, requires little water, and produces a hypoallergenic latex. The rubber it yields is of such high quality that it can replace synthetic rubber in many tyre components. Michelin, supplier to WEC and Formula E, is experimenting with guayule and also with rubber sourced from sustainably managed hevea plantations.

Recycled and Bio‑Sourced Materials

Modern race tyres incorporate recycled steel for the belts and recycled carbon black recovered from end‑of‑life tyres. Some compounds now use silica derived from rice husk ash – an agricultural waste product – instead of mined sand. The goal is a tyre that is 100% renewable or recycled by 2030. Formula E’s Gen3 tyres are already 26% renewable or recycled, and the percentage increases each season.

Zero‑Waste Manufacturing & End‑of‑Life

Pirelli’s motorsport tyre factories have achieved zero waste to landfill. Used race tyres are shredded and turned into playground surfaces, running tracks, or fuel for cement kilns. The FIA’s environmental accreditation requires circuits to have a tyre recycling plan. The ultimate aim is a fully circular tyre – one that can be retreaded or chemically recycled into new tyres indefinitely.

Road Tyres Follow Racing’s Lead

The eco‑focused compounds developed for racing are already appearing in consumer tyres. Pirelli’s “Green Performance” road tyres use the same natural rubber and silica technologies. Michelin’s “Vision” concept tyre is bio‑degradable and 3D‑printed – a direct spin‑off from its WEC program. Racing proves that sustainable tyres can perform at the highest level, and the market is responding.
