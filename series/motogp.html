MotoGP – Two Wheels, Zero Compromise

The world’s fastest motorcycle championship is embracing sustainable fuels, electric support classes, and a vision of a carbon‑neutral grid.

Sustainable Fuel Roadmap (2024 → 2027)

MotoGP has committed to a gradual switch to non‑fossil fuels. From 2024, all bikes must run on at least 40% non‑fossil fuel. By 2027, that will rise to 100% sustainable fuel, made from e‑fuels or advanced biofuels. The fuel is being developed by a consortium including Petronas and Repsol, with the explicit goal of creating a fuel that can also be sold to the public for everyday motorcycles.

MotoE: The Electric World Cup

Since 2019, MotoE has served as the all‑electric support series for MotoGP. The current Ducati V21L bike reaches 270 km/h and can complete a full race distance on a single charge. The batteries are fast‑swapped between sessions and charged using renewable energy. MotoE is a testbed for lightweight electric powertrains that could power future urban commuter bikes.

Lightweight Efficiency

Motorcycles are inherently efficient, but MotoGP pushes that to an extreme. The bikes weigh just 157 kg and produce over 300 hp from 1000 cc engines – a power‑to‑weight ratio that surpasses F1. The pursuit of lightness has driven advances in titanium, magnesium, and carbon fibre that have trickled down to production superbikes, making them faster and more fuel‑efficient.

Future Technologies

  • Hydrogen combustion: Prototype hydrogen‑powered motorcycles have been demonstrated by Kawasaki and Yamaha, using modified internal combustion engines with zero CO₂ emissions. MotoGP is exploring a future hydrogen class.
  • Bio‑composites: Fairings made from flax fibre and recycled carbon are being tested, reducing the environmental footprint of bodywork.
  • Tyre sustainability: Michelin is working on a tyre that uses 50% renewable or recycled materials by 2025, with a full‑sustainable tyre targeted for 2030.

MotoGP demonstrates that even the most visceral, high‑revving sport can transition to a sustainable future without losing its soul.
