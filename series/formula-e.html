Formula E – The Electric Revolution on City Streets

Net‑zero since day one, Formula E is proving that zero‑emission racing can be fast, exciting, and a catalyst for urban e‑mobility.

All‑Electric, All‑Action

Formula E was launched in 2014 as the world’s first all‑electric single‑seater championship. Races take place on temporary street circuits in the heart of major cities – from London to São Paulo – bringing the excitement of electric mobility directly to urban audiences. The series is a genuine testbed for EV technology, constantly pushing the limits of battery density, motor efficiency, and fast charging.

Today’s Gen3 car produces 600 kW (over 800 hp) total power from a 350 kW rear motor and a 250 kW front motor used exclusively for regeneration. It’s the most efficient racing car ever built: over 90% of the energy drawn from the battery reaches the wheels, compared to around 40% for a petrol engine. The car has no rear hydraulic brakes – all deceleration is handled by the electric motors, recovering energy that would otherwise be lost.

Net‑Zero Carbon from Day One

Formula E is the only global sport to have been certified as net‑zero carbon since its inception. This applies to the entire championship: cars, logistics, events, and fan travel. Emissions that cannot be eliminated are offset through certified projects. The series also ensures that every race tyre is recycled and that waste at events is minimised.

Battery Innovation: Second Life & Fast Charging

The Gen3 battery is a structural component of the car, saving weight and improving safety. It can be fast‑charged at 600 kW during pit stops, adding 4 kWh in just 30 seconds – enough for the final sprint of the race. This ultra‑fast charging technology is being transferred to consumer EVs, influencing the next generation of 800‑volt architectures from Porsche, Hyundai, and others.

After their racing life, Formula E batteries enter a second‑life program. They are repurposed for solar micro‑grids in off‑grid communities and for grid storage, providing clean energy to schools and hospitals. The cells are eventually recycled, recovering over 95% of the valuable metals.

Series‑Specific Sustainability Initiatives

  • All‑renewable energy: Every Formula E event is powered by 100% renewable electricity, often generated on‑site with solar arrays and battery storage.
  • Sustainable logistics: The championship uses DHL’s GoGreen service, offsetting all freight emissions and prioritising sea freight over air freight where possible.
  • Fan engagement: The “FIA Girls on Track” initiative encourages young women into STEM, and the “Attack Mode” fan vote allows spectators to directly influence the race – the first interactive sustainable motorsport experience.

Influencing the EV Revolution

Formula E’s high‑profile city events have made electric cars aspirational. The technology tested on track – inverters, cooling systems, battery management software – is licensed to road car manufacturers. Jaguar Land Rover, Nissan, and DS Automobiles openly use Formula E to accelerate their production EV programs. The series has avoided over 100,000 tonnes of CO₂ compared to a traditional racing series of similar scale.
