Extreme E – Racing with a Purpose

Electric SUVs racing in the most damaged ecosystems on Earth, using sport to accelerate environmental action.

The Mission: Sport for Climate Action

Extreme E isn’t just a racing series; it’s a social enterprise. The championship takes electric SUV racing to remote locations already impacted by climate change – the Saudi Arabian desert, Greenland’s retreating ice cap, the Amazon rainforest, and the coastal mangroves of Senegal. Each event is designed to raise awareness about a specific environmental issue and to leave a positive legacy.

Pioneering Hydrogen Charging

Because the race locations are far from any electrical grid, the entire paddock is powered by a mobile hydrogen fuel cell system. Green hydrogen, produced using solar energy, is brought to the site and converted into electricity by fuel cells. This not only powers the race cars but also all support infrastructure. It’s a working proof‑of‑concept for off‑grid, zero‑emission energy – a model that can be used for disaster relief, construction, and remote communities.

Legacy Projects: Leaving It Better

Every race location receives a legacy project: in Greenland, Extreme E supported a climate‑education program for local youth; in Senegal, it planted mangroves to combat coastal erosion; in the Amazon, it funded reforestation and agroforestry training. These are not token gestures – they are long‑term commitments backed by scientific partners like the University of Oxford.

The Car: All‑Electric, All‑Purpose

The Odyssey 21 car produces 550 hp from twin electric motors, accelerates to 100 km/h in 4.5 seconds, and tackles gradients of up to 130%. Built with natural‑fibre bodywork and recycled materials, it’s designed to be as sustainable as it is capable. The car is a shared platform – all teams use identical vehicles, proving that performance can come from efficiency and software, not just endless development budgets.

Extreme E proves that motorsport can be a force for good, turning a race weekend into a catalyst for real environmental change.
