IndyCar – American Speed Meets Clean Fuel

From the Indy 500 to the streets of Long Beach, IndyCar is proving that renewable fuel and hybrid tech can power the world’s fastest oval racing.

100% Renewable Race Fuel (2023)

Since the 2023 season, every IndyCar has run on Shell 100% Renewable Race Fuel. This advanced biofuel is made from sugarcane waste and other biomass, reducing lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions by at least 60%. It’s a drop‑in fuel, meaning the existing 2.2‑litre twin‑turbo V6 engines required zero modifications. Over the course of a season, the switch saves over 15,000 tonnes of CO₂ compared to fossil fuel.

Hybrid Debut (2024)

IndyCar’s long‑awaited hybrid system debuted in 2024. Unlike F1’s battery‑based ERS, IndyCar uses a supercapacitor paired with an MGU (Motor Generator Unit) mounted to the gearbox. The supercapacitor charges and discharges in a fraction of a second, making it ideal for the short, intense bursts needed for overtaking. The hybrid provides around 60 hp of additional boost, improving lap times and fuel efficiency simultaneously.

The choice of a supercapacitor over a lithium‑ion battery is deliberate: it lasts longer, is lighter, and doesn’t require complex cooling. This technology is being evaluated for future mild‑hybrid road cars, where frequent stop‑start and rapid energy recovery are essential.

Sustainability Across the Series

  • Green tyres: Firestone provides tyres made with recycled materials and is developing a fully sustainable race tyre.
  • Logistics: IndyCar consolidates freight and uses biofuel‑powered transporters for the European rounds.
  • Community: The series partners with local organisations at each race to promote recycling and clean energy.

IndyCar proves that even the most traditional, high‑speed series can embrace cutting‑edge sustainability without losing the spectacle.
